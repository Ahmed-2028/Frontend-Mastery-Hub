 ### 📘 Chapter 3: Variables Mastery
> **Concept:** Variables are the storage units of programming. Think of them as labeled boxes where you can store, update, and retrieve data.

---

#### 📜 Essential Rules (The Logic)
1. **Declaration (`let`):**
   * Before using a variable, you must declare it using the `let` keyword. This tells the computer to reserve a spot in memory.
2. **Assignment (`=`):**
   * The `=` operator is used for assignment, meaning "take the value on the right and store it in the variable on the left."
3. **Reassignment (Updating):**
   * Variables are dynamic. You can update their value at any time, and the computer will only remember the most recent value.
4. **Value vs. Literal (The Golden Rule):**
   * **`fish`** (No quotes): Refers to the **Value** stored inside the variable.
   * **`"fish"`** (With quotes): Refers to the **Text** literal "fish" itself.
